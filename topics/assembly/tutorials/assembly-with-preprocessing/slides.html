The DNA is fragmented and adapters are added. Each nanowell contains oligonucleotides that provide an anchoring point for the adaptors. 2. The DNA strand bends and attaches to the oligo that is complementary to the top adapter sequence. 3. A primer attaches to the forward strands adapter primer binding site. 4. Polymerase adds a fluorescently tagged dNTP to the DNA strand. Each of the four bases has a unique emission, which allows to identify which base has been incorporated. 5. The now double stranded DNA is denatured so that each strand can separately attach to an oligonucleotide sequence anchored to the flow cell. 6. Over and over again, DNA strands will bend and attach to the solid support. ] --- .enlarge200[ # **Data sources: Nanopore sequencing** ] ![](../../images/nanopore_sequencing_process.png) ---
